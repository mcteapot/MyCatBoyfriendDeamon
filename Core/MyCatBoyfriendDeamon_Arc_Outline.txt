Arc Outline

My Cat Boyfriend Is the Town Demon

ARC 1 — THE SHRINE MARK
Bell: Bell 1 — The Hunger Bell
Minor Villain: Jikininki corpse demons / hungry revenants
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Mina Park
New Characters Introduced: Kuro Aonuma, Mina Park, Ren Shirogane as a shadowed watcher
Arc Motivation: Hana is drawn to the mysterious new transfer student everyone is talking about, follows him after school, and accidentally witnesses the supernatural truth hidden beneath the town.
Arc-End Twist: Hana and Kuro’s first physical/spiritual connection releases trapped spirits from the shrine, creating the chain reaction that drives the rest of the season.

Arc 1 opens in full crisis: Hana is already hiding inside the abandoned shrine, terrified, while Jikininki corpse demons crawl toward her. Kuro, the mysterious new transfer student, fights them off in his full chained blue cat-demon form, revealing himself as something beautiful, dangerous, and inhuman. After he defeats the Jikininki, the final corpse demon transforms into the first blue Moonbell, which slams into Kuro’s collar and causes a painful, charged spiritual awakening. Hana runs, but Kuro corners her against the shrine wall in a kabedon moment, trapping her with one clawed hand beside her head; the scene is frightening, intimate, and electric.

The arc then cuts back to earlier that day, showing Kuro’s arrival at school on his loud bosozoku-style motorcycle. He instantly becomes the talk of the entire school: students film him, whisper about his look, and speculate that he transferred because of something dangerous. Mina Park is introduced here as Hana’s stylish, funny best friend, texting Hana about “the new boy in town” and teasing her when Hana seems unusually affected by him. Hana feels strangely pulled toward Kuro, especially when her red shrine bracelet reacts as he walks past her. After school, she sees him leave alone and secretly follows him to the shrine.

When we return to the shrine, Kuro embraces Hana after the kabedon, and their contact forces him out of full demon form into a cat-human form. The embrace is charged and intimate, but it also detonates a massive blue spirit explosion from beneath the shrine. This explosion releases trapped spirits into the town, which becomes the real start of the season’s supernatural outbreak. Ren is introduced only as a shadowed figure watching from outside the shrine, horrified by what he sees, carrying a black violin case but not yet revealing who he is. At dawn, Hana and Kuro walk back toward school connected by a visible spiritual chain from his collar to her hand, creating a strangely cute “cat-human pet leash” image before sunlight dissolves the chain. The arc ends with the bond still marked on Hana’s hand, while a student-shaped figure watches them from the school shadows and turns to reveal it has no face: the Noppera-bō has followed them back.

ARC 2 — THE FACELESS GIRL
Bell: Bell 2 — The Face Bell
Minor Villain: Noppera-bō, a faceless identity-stealing yokai
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Mina Park, Saki Hoshino, Ren Shirogane
New Characters Introduced: Saki Hoshino, Principal Hoshino, Ren Shirogane properly revealed
Arc Motivation: The spirits released from the shrine begin invading school life, and Hana must protect Mina while hiding her bond with Kuro from the student body.
Arc-End Twist: Ren reveals himself as a Spirit Warden, claims Kuro is the cause of the spirit outbreak, and tries to kill him while pulling Hana into his protection.

Arc 2 moves the supernatural threat into the school, where the Noppera-bō begins feeding on identity, popularity, and public image. Hoshino is introduced as a popular livestreamer with a sharp, beautiful, socially cruel “Mean Girls” / “Heathers” energy. She is a status predator who knows how to turn gossip into entertainment, and she immediately notices Hana’s strange connection to Kuro. Saki starts livestreaming rumors about the mysterious new transfer student and Hana, framing them as scandalous while pretending she is only “asking questions.” The larger threat is hinted at when the school principal, Principal Hoshino, appears and calmly protects Saki’s behavior instead of stopping it, suggesting the adult world is already invested in controlling the story.

The Noppera-bō possesses Mina, using Hana’s best friend as its emotional weapon. Possessed Mina says things Hana is afraid to hear, accusing her of being obsessed with Kuro and abandoning her friends for a boy she barely knows. Kuro and Hana are forced to work together to save Mina without hurting her, which deepens their bond because Hana sees that Kuro’s violence has limits when innocent people are involved. The Noppera-bō attacks internally and socially rather than just physically: it steals faces, mimics voices, and turns school gossip into a weapon. When Hana and Kuro finally defeat it, Mina’s stolen face peels away from the spirit, and the Noppera-bō transforms into the second blue Moonbell. The bell flies into Kuro’s collar, triggering another painful, sensual awakening that Hana watches with fear and fascination.

At the end of the arc, Ren steps in for the first time as a visible player, carrying his black violin case. He does not yet reveal the Silver Requiem Violin; the case itself is enough to create tension. Ren confronts Kuro and moves to kill him, claiming Kuro is the source of the shrine outbreak and all the missing-person trouble in town. He reveals that he belongs to the Spirit Wardens, a secret order that hunts and seals supernatural threats. Hana tries to stop him, and Ren pulls her into his arms in a romantic, protective moment, telling her that he can still save her if she walks away from Kuro now. The arc ends with Hana trapped between Kuro, who she is spiritually bound to, and Ren, who claims he is the only one who knows what Kuro really is.

ARC 3 — THE HALF CRAWLING SPIRIT
Bell: Bell 3 — The Severance Bell
Minor Villain: Teke Teke / crawling school spirit
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Saki Hoshino
New Character / Presence Introduced: Aoi appears for the first time as a supernatural apparition through Hana’s body
Arc Motivation: Hana tries to hold together the fragile triangle after Ren’s attack on Kuro, but the school becomes dangerous again when a crawling spirit begins hunting students connected to the shrine rumors.
Arc-End Twist: After Kuro defeats the crawling spirit and receives the third bell, Hana’s unconscious body briefly becomes Aoi in Kuro’s arms, revealing to Kuro and Ren that Hana’s connection to Kuro comes from another lifetime.

Arc 3 opens by resolving the confrontation at the end of Arc 2. Ren is ready to kill Kuro, convinced that Kuro caused the shrine outbreak and that Hana is being manipulated by the curse. Hana steps between them and talks Ren down, refusing to let him hurt Kuro but also unable to fully reject Ren because he is her long-time friend and still feels like safety. Ren reluctantly backs off, but he warns Hana that the Spirit Wardens will not ignore what Kuro has done, and that if she keeps protecting him, she may be treated as part of the threat. This leaves Hana emotionally torn: Kuro is the one she is spiritually bound to, but Ren is the boy she has trusted for years.

The school threat escalates when a Teke Teke-style crawling spirit begins stalking the hallways after students spread rumors about Hana, Kuro, and the missing people near the shrine. Saki Hoshino feeds the panic through her livestream presence, treating the disappearances and Hana’s connection to Kuro like entertainment, which makes the spirit stronger. The crawling spirit attacks in violent bursts, dragging itself along floors, ceilings, and stairwells, targeting students who repeat the shrine rumors. Hana sees Saki in real danger and chooses to help her despite Saki’s cruelty. Hana pulls Saki out of the spirit’s path, but Saki panics and runs away, shaken by the fact that Hana saved her.

The battle reaches its peak when the Teke Teke knocks Hana to the ground and leaves her unconscious. As the crawling spirit closes in to kill her, Ren arrives first and finally reveals the truth of his weapon system. He opens the black violin case and draws the Silver Requiem Violin, using its music to freeze the spirit for a few seconds. When the spirit breaks through the sound, Ren’s violin bow transforms into the Silver Saber, and he uses it to block the killing strike. Kuro then arrives in a burst of blue flame and attacks with full feral force, driving the creature back while Ren holds its movement in place.

Kuro delivers the final blow, banishing the crawling spirit with blue flame. The Teke Teke’s severed body collapses into crawling sparks and twisting strips of shadow that reform into the third blue Moonbell. The bell slams into Kuro’s collar, triggering another painful, sensual awakening: his collar burns blue, his body arches with spiritual force, and his cat-demon traits pulse through him as the bell locks into place. The victory is charged because Ren has now fully revealed his exorcist power, while Kuro has proven again that he is the one who lands the decisive blow when Hana is in danger.

After the bell locks into place, Kuro finds Hana unconscious on the ground and lifts her into his arms to check if she is alive. The moment he holds her, the world shifts for Kuro and Ren — but not for Hana, because she is knocked out. Hana’s body becomes Aoi in Kuro’s arms, and the school hallway transforms into a feudal-era battlefield with a burning castle, smoke, screams, and chaos behind them. Aoi clutches Kuro and confesses that she loves him, terrified that they are doomed to keep finding and losing each other across time. Ren sees the apparition too, which horrifies him because it proves Hana’s bond with Kuro is older than the current curse and far deeper than simple attraction. When the vision ends, Hana wakes with no memory of what happened, leaving Kuro shaken, Ren frightened, and the next arc set up around the question of who Aoi was — and why Hana’s soul keeps returning to Kuro.


ARC 4 — EMI’S HOUSE / THE MIRROR CAT BREACH
Bell: Bell 4 — The False Reflection Bell, released from the Mirror Cat’s first mirror-core
Minor Villain: Mirror Cat / Bakeneko reflection spirit
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Emi Mori
New Character Introduced: Emi Mori
Arc Motivation: Ren and Kuro must stop fighting long enough to save the injured Hana, forcing them into Hana’s protected family space where the truth about Aoi, Kuro’s lost memories, and the old seal begins to surface.
Arc-End Twist: Emi’s confession about Aoi opens a spiritual breach, allowing the Mirror Cat to cross into the house; Emi and Ren crack its first mirror-core and release the False Reflection Bell, but the spirit escapes at dawn with its second mirror-core intact.

Arc 4 begins immediately after the apparition of Aoi, with Hana injured and unconscious in Kuro’s arms. Ren is shaken by what he saw, but he forces himself to focus and tells Kuro that Hana needs spiritual healing, not a hospital. The only person who can help her is Hana’s grandmother, Emi Mori, a wise but plucky old woman with deep shrine knowledge. Kuro does not trust Ren, and Ren still believes Kuro is dangerous, but Hana’s condition forces them to cooperate. The motivation of the arc is simple and urgent: get Hana to Emi before the curse damage spreads.

Ren[d] leads[e] Kuro to Emi’s home, but the house is protected by strong spiritual wards that violently stop Kuro at the entrance. The wards recognize him as a demon and force him back, burning blue against his collar and making him almost feral with frustration because Hana is inside and he cannot reach her. Ren, surprisingly, backs Kuro up and tells Emi that Kuro helped save Hana, even if he still does not trust him. Emi enters as a sharp, funny, no-nonsense grandmother[f] who immediately sees through both boys’ posturing and tells them to stop growling at each other like alley cats. In a cute cat-like ritual, Emi sprays Kuro with a blessed bottle of water, scolds him to behave, and temporarily adjusts the wards so he can enter her home without being rejected.

Inside Emi’s house, the energy shifts from action to tense recovery. Emi treats the spiritual injury on Hana’s hand, and Kuro stays close, visibly unsettled by how protective he feels. Hana recovers and wakes up after Emi’s treatment, which means she is present and conscious when the deeper confession begins. Ren starts questioning Emi about the apparition they saw after the battle: Hana becoming Aoi in Kuro’s arms, the burning castle, and Aoi’s confession of love. Hana is confused because she remembers none of it, but she immediately understands that Ren and Kuro both saw something involving her body that she did not experience.

Emi tries to deflect at first, but Ren presses her, and Kuro’s reaction makes it clear that something in him recognizes the name even though he cannot remember why. Emi is finally forced to confess in front of all three of them that Aoi is tied to the Mori bloodline and that Hana is not simply descended from her — Hana carries the same soul returning through time. Hana hears this directly and is shaken by the idea that her feelings, her mark, and her connection to Kuro may have begun before she was born. Emi reveals that Aoi was the shrine maiden who sealed Kuro and took away his memories. Kuro is devastated by the revelation because he has spent centuries with missing memories and pain he never understood.

Emi admits her family line has been tasked with guarding the truth, preserving the wards, and preparing for the day Hana and Kuro would find each other again. She insists that what Aoi did was not cruelty for its own sake; it was done to stop something worse from escaping beneath the shrine. This creates a major emotional rupture: Kuro feels betrayed by Aoi and by Hana’s bloodline, Ren feels vindicated that Kuro is tied to a dangerous ancient seal, and Hana feels violated by a past life she cannot remember but is apparently still paying for. The reveal makes the triangle more painful because Ren sees Hana as someone he must protect from an ancient pattern, while Kuro now fears looking at Hana and seeing Aoi.

At the height of the confession, the Mirror Cat crosses into Emi’s home through a reflective surface, using the spiritual instability caused by Aoi’s name and Kuro’s fractured memory. It creates confusion by showing everyone warped false reflections born from fear: Kuro sees a version of himself with no collar, Ren sees Hana turning away from him, Hana sees Aoi’s face replacing her own, and Emi sees Aoi repeating the sealing ritual. Kuro is the most shaken because he nearly sees his own true reflection and freezes, unable to act. Ren snaps out of the illusion first and uses his revealed weapons with purpose, drawing the Silver Requiem Violin and Silver Saber to fight the spirit inside the house. Emi works beside him with shrine wards and old paper charms, proving she is not just a lore keeper but an active spiritual defender.

Ren and Emi manage to repel the Mirror Cat, forcing it back across the threshold before it can reach Hana again. Their combined silver music and shrine wards crack the spirit’s first mirror-core, and the broken glass-light twists into the fourth Moonbell: the False Reflection Bell. The bell flies into Kuro’s collar, causing a partial awakening that feels incomplete and wrong, as if only one side of the reflection has been restored. Emi restores the house wards, and the wounded spirit is knocked outside, trapped beyond the protective boundary as dawn begins to break. The Mirror Cat pauses in the early light, staring at Kuro, Hana, Ren, and Emi as if it has learned exactly what each of them fears. Then it hisses like a cat and runs off into the morning shadows with its second mirror-core still intact. The arc ends with Hana now fully aware of the Aoi revelation, Kuro emotionally shattered by the truth that Aoi sealed him, and the Mirror Cat loose in town carrying the second half of the Reflection Bell — setting up the next arc’s attack on identity, memory, and forbidden desire.


ARC 5 — THE MIRROR SCHOOL
Bell: Bell 5 — The True Reflection Bell
Minor Villain: Mirror Cat / Bakeneko reflection spirit
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Mina Park
New Character / Presence Introduced: The Mirror Cat’s false versions of Hana, Kuro, Ren, and Aoi
Arc Motivation: The Mirror Cat escapes Emi’s wards and turns the school into a psychological trap, forcing Hana, Kuro, Ren, and Mina to confront distorted versions of the people they trust and desire.
Arc-End Problem: The Mirror Cat’s second defeat adds the True Reflection Bell to Kuro’s collar, but the awakening visibly makes him less human, raising the question of whether saving Kuro[g] is actually restoring him — or turning him into something more dangerous.

The gang returns to school after Emi’s confession, but the atmosphere is wrong from the start. Mirrors, windows, phone screens, trophy cases, and even puddles begin showing warped reflections of students: prettier, crueler, older, dead, faceless, or monstrous versions of themselves. After losing its false mirror-core at Emi’s house, the Mirror Cat becomes more seductive and precise: it no longer shows only what people fear, but what they secretly want to believe. Mina becomes one of its first targets when an evil reflection of Hana appears in a classroom window and tells her that Hana has replaced her with Kuro and Ren. The attack wounds Mina emotionally and makes Hana realize the spirit knows exactly which relationships to twist.

The Mirror Cat then targets Kuro by appearing as a free, uncollared version of himself. Thus confrontation happens in the school yard in the day past noon, Kuno has not power during the da. This false Kuro whispers that Ren wants Hana for himself, that Hana only sees Kuro as a monster to save, and that every bell added to the collar is turning him back into the weapon Aoi created. Hana sees Kuro spiraling and tries to snap him out of it, but the spirit changes tactics by appearing as a tender false Kuro who says everything Hana secretly wants to hear. Hana nearly kisses the fake version before realizing something is off, and the real Kuro sees the moment, causing his jealousy and demon instincts to explode. His collar burns, his cat-demon traits surge, and for a moment, Hana cannot tell whether he is protecting her or about to lose control.

Ren arrives at the last moment and uses the Silver Requiem Violin and Silver Saber to destroy the Mirror Cat’s second reflective core. Hana understands the clever difference between the two defeats: at Emi’s house, they broke the false reflection by rejecting fear; at school, they break the true reflection by refusing a comforting lie. The spirit shatters into glass-like fragments, then reforms into the fifth Moonbell: the True Reflection Bell. When the bell flies into Kuro’s collar, the payoff is more disturbing than before: the transformation causes visible distress to both Kuro and Hana because their bond makes her feel part of his pain. Kuro’s body pulses with blue light, his eyes become more animal, his hands sharpen briefly into claws, and his voice drops into something less human before he forces himself back. Hana watches, shaken and drawn to him, but now frightened by the cost.

After the bell locks into place, Ren turns to Hana and offers her the safer path. He tells her that if the bells continue returning to Kuro, there may be nothing human left to save. He offers to remove her mark and rod of Kuro before she loses herself to him completely. This puts Hana in a painful position: Kuro is the one she feels bound to, but Ren may be right that the bond is awakening something dangerous. The arc should end with a strong emotional and supernatural turn that sets up the next arc’s escalation. Also, the wardens storm in at this moment.


ARC 6 — THE WARDEN’S SONG
Bell: Bell 6 — The Requiem Bell
Minor Villain: Biwa-bokuboku / corrupted instrument tsukumogami
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Daigo Shirogane, Principal Hoshino, masked Spirit Wardens
New Characters Introduced: Daigo Shirogane, the masked Spirit Wardens, Principal Hoshino as an active conspiratorial adult presence
Arc Motivation: The Spirit Wardens arrive to eliminate Kuro and take control of Hana, but a corrupted music spirit interrupts the execution attempt and forces Kuro and Ren into humiliating cooperation.
Arc-End Twist: Daigo determines that Hana is not merely cursed or endangered — under Warden law, she is now a living seal component.

Arc 6 begins when Principal Hoshino calls in the Spirit Wardens and urges them to “do their job,” revealing that the school’s adult authority is actively cooperating with the supernatural power structure. The Wardens surround Hana, Kuro, and Ren in the school courtyard, and Daigo Shirogane steps forward as their leader. Daigo is coldly disapproving of Ren, openly criticizing him for failing to dispatch Kuro earlier and allowing Hana’s bond to deepen. As Daigo prepares to kill Kuro himself, Hana pleads for them to stop, but Principal Hoshino watches with quiet, evil satisfaction, enjoying the fact that the situation is turning against Kuro. Hana’s mark suddenly reacts under the pressure, and spiritual chains erupt from her hand to Kuro’s collar, forcing Kuro into a leashed demon form in front of everyone. The Wardens panic because the display proves Hana has direct influence over the collar system.

Before Daigo can strike, everything goes unnaturally quiet, and a strange music beat begins building from the abandoned music room. The Biwa-bokuboku, a corrupted instrument spirit, reveals itself at the center of the courtyard, drawn by Ren’s violin, Kuro’s collar, and the Wardens. When Daigo and the Wardens move to intercept it, the spirit plays a single violent note that blasts them backward and shatters every courtyard window. The standoff is forced to pause, not because the Wardens show mercy, but because the music spirit is now the immediate threat. Its melody hits Kuro and Ren at the same time, and both of their bodies respond against their will. The Biwa-bokuboku uses corrupted music like puppet strings, forcing them into movement while feeding on their humiliation and resistance.

Kuro fights the rhythm with raw strength, blue flame, and feral rage, but he cannot stop his feet from moving. Ren recognizes the technique as a warped version of Spirit Warden command music, but even he cannot override it. The spirit forces the two rivals into a stylized, humiliating, viral-dance-like battle in the courtyard, making their bodies move in sharp, rhythmic patterns while the Wardens watch in shock. Hana is horrified at first, then notices the rule: the spirit’s hold gets stronger whenever Kuro and Ren resist in opposite directions. When they fight each other’s rhythm, the music gets louder, and their movements become more violent. Hana shouts that they have to stop resisting each other and match the beat instead.

Kuro and Ren hate the idea, but they do it because Hana is right. Ren uses the Silver Requiem Violin to play a counter-rhythm, trying to break the corrupted melody. Kuro has to move with the rhythm instead of against it, using his body like a weapon while the dance steps redirect the spirit’s control back at itself. Kuro’s feral physicality and Ren’s polished precision finally moving in sync. As the counter-rhythm weakens the Biwa-bokuboku, Kuro and Ren step into the same beat together, and Kuro lands the final banishing strike through blue flame. The spirit’s instrument body cracks open, its strings coil into blue light, and it transforms into the sixth Moonbell.

The bell locks into Kuro’s collar with a violent chime. The cost plays out in full: blue light burns under his skin, his eyes go animal, his claws flash out, and his demon traits surge before he drags himself back into human form. Hana steadies him, and the intimacy of that moment is impossible to hide; she is frightened by what the bells are doing to him, but she is also drawn to the raw spiritual awakening in his body. [h]Daigo studies Hana’s glowing mark and the way Kuro responds to her touch, then delivers his finding without drama: under Spirit Warden law, Hana is a seal component. Ren does not argue. Hana looks to him for but[i] Ren looks away.

The Wardens withdraw because they cannot take Hana without Ren’s cooperation, and Ren will not give it yet. Daigo leaves Ren with one instruction: find out how many bells remain and report back. Ren agrees, but Hana does not hear the full weight of that promise. Kuro does understand it, and he stays quiet, realizing Ren has not fully chosen Hana over the Wardens. As Daigo reaches the school gate, he turns back and says directly to Hana, “You are no longer the victim. You are part of the seal.” In the window behind him, a black-red shadow watches with too many eyes.


ARC 7 — SPIDER AT THE BELL FESTIVAL
Bell: Bell 7 — The Thread Bell
Minor Villain: Jorōgumo, spider woman yokai disguised as the festival play’s narrator
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Mina Park
New Character / Presence Introduced: The Jorōgumo performer; Mawari as a mythic figure inside the town’s festival play
Arc Motivation: The cast attends the romantic Bell Festival hoping for one normal night, but the festival’s traditional theater performance reveals that the town’s beloved legend is actually an allegory for Mawari’s rise.
Arc-End Twist: The Jorōgumo’s defeat restores the Thread Bell to Kuro’s collar, but the festival play’s depiction of Mawari is not just folklore — the real Mawari is watching from a distant mountainside shrine.

Arc 7 begins at the Bell Festival, shifting the story into a romantic, playful public setting before turning it into a supernatural trap. Hana arrives with Kuro, Ren, and Mina, and the festival lets the love triangle breathe: Hana has cute, tense moments with Kuro as his collar reacts to the bells around the festival stalls, forcing her to control him with a funny, Inuyasha-style “down boy” command when his cat-demon instincts flare. The control is playful at first, but it also reminds everyone that Hana’s bond with Kuro is real and physical, not just emotional. Ren uses the festival’s softer atmosphere to get close to Hana, explaining more of his ties to the Spirit Wardens and trying to make her understand why they fear Kuro. He nearly confesses his feelings, and Hana is visibly torn because Ren is familiar, loyal, and wounded in a way she cannot dismiss, even though she resists giving him the answer he wants.

The second half of the arc moves into the festival’s traditional theater performance, a stylized mix of kabuki and Nō theater. Hana excitedly pulls Kuro through the entrance, telling him this is her favorite part of the festival because it tells the story of the town’s history. Kuro listens with surprising softness, almost purring beside her, because the bells, masks, drums, and old language stir something ancient in him. Everyone takes their seats, and the play begins as a mythic story about a shadow beneath the mountain shrine, a guardian beast, a maiden, a silver musician, and a village that survived by telling a beautiful lie. The audience thinks they are watching folklore, but the story is actually an allegory of Mawari’s rise, with eerie echoes of Hana, Kuro, Ren, and Mina built into the roles.

The Jorōgumo reveals herself through the performance as the spider-like narrator controlling the story from the center of the stage. At first, her control looks like theater magic: actors move with perfect timing, sleeves snap like webs, masks turn in unison, and red threads shimmer under the stage lights. Then audience members begin standing up and moving as if they are part of the play, controlled by invisible silk. The Jorōgumo turns the entire theater into a web, using the festival legend to trap everyone inside the town’s false history. Mina being controlled is the breaking point: the spirit puppets her body and forces her toward Hana with a ceremonial blade, turning Hana’s best friend into a weapon.

Hana breaks out of the trance and sees through the web of lies spun by the spider and the at the last moment and dodges the attack and then forces Ren to wake up by shoving his instrument case at him. He snaps out of it and sees the threads connecting the actors, audience, stage curtains, and rafters, realizing they are all inside the Jorōgumo’s web. Hana commands Ren to free Kuno. With a sharp note from the Silver Requiem Violin and sets Kuro free, then slices open enough of the web for Hana to release Kuro’s demon form. Hana commands Kuro to attack. Kuro charges the Jorōgumo across the stage while she lashes him with spider silk, binding his arms, collar, and throat. Ren supports[j] him from the floor with precise violin notes, cutting webs away each time Kuro is trapped, forcing the two rivals into clean, urgent teamwork. Finally, Kuro breaks through and delivers the decisive blow, tearing through the Jorōgumo’s center as all the red threads snap across the theater.

The Jorōgumo’s body dissolves into black silk, stage ribbons, and blue sparks, all twisting together into the seventh Moonbell. The bell flies into Kuro’s collar on the theater stage, triggering a charged, almost uncontrollable transformation in front of Hana. Blue light crawls across his throat and chest, his eyes go fully animal, his claws extend, and the bells on his collar ring out of rhythm as if something inside him is trying to wake up too fast. Hana rushes to him and holds him close, soothing him through the bond before he loses himself completely. The moment is intimate and dangerous: Kuro is trembling with power, Hana is the only thing anchoring him, and Ren is forced to watch as her closeness pulls Kuro back from the edge.

The arc ends far away from the festival, at a mountainside shrine under the moon. A black-red spirit shadow appears, shaped like the terrifying figure from the festival play’s Mawari legend, not fully formed but clearly alive. It watches the moon as the final echoes of the Thread Bell ring from the theater below. Then the shadow wisps upward and shoots into the moonlight, suggesting that the play did not merely tell Mawari’s story — it awakened part of him.


ARC 8 — THE BONE KAIJU BROADCAST
Bell: Bell 8 — The Bone Bell, hidden inside the Gashadokuro and not yet recovered
Minor Villain: Gashadokuro, giant skeleton yokai formed from the town’s missing dead
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Mina Park, Saki Hoshino
New Characters / Elements Introduced: Aoi speaking directly through Hana; Ren’s Spirit Warden mecha-summon power; Spirit Warden orbital HQ / silver zord system
Arc Motivation: Saki’s livestream turns town gossip into public spectacle, but the mockery and lies trigger a supernatural kaiju-level escalation that forces Hana, Kuro, and Ren toward the shrine.
Arc-End Twist: Ren’s silver mecha is impaled by the Gashadokuro, leaving the giant skeleton free to follow Hana and Kuro toward the shrine.

Arc 8 shifts tone hard into a livestream-driven, kaiju-tokusatsu escalation. The arc begins through Saki’s TikTok livestream as she wanders the school with her phone, talking to chat, stirring gossip, and reframing the theater incident as “mass food poisoning” from the yakitori stand run by Mina’s family. Her chat reacts with chaotic short-form comments: some viewers call it fake, some accuse Hana of being involved, some thirst over Kuro, and others spam different reactions. Saki pushes Kuro first, trying to get him on camera, but he reacts like a hostile cat — hissing, glaring, and refusing to play along — which only makes her audience more excited. Cut she then corners Ren and starts digging into his relationship with Hana, asking why the perfect orchestra boy cares about “some shrine-girl nobody,” and even presses him about why the Spirit Wardens have not “cleaned up” the town yet. Before Ren can answer, Mina storms in and gets in Saki’s face for blaming her father’s festival food stand, nearly swinging at her and making the livestream explode with comments.

The arc then cuts to dusk outside a convenience store called Neko Mart, a slightly goofy local knockoff with a smiling cat mascot on the sign. Saki finds Hana there and pushes the livestream directly into her face, demanding to know whether Hana is hooking up with Kuro, leading Ren on, or playing both of them for attention. Saki frames it as public accountability, saying the town deserves answers because people are missing and Hana keeps ending up at the center of every disaster. As Saki’s questions get sharper, the livestream background begins changing: clouds darken, the sky turns bruised red, and a red beam or wisp of light descends behind Hana. The chat shifts from gossip to panic, with comments asking whether the sky is part of the stream filter, whether Saki is staging this, and why the clouds look like they are bleeding. Saki keeps talking for a beat too long, not realizing her own livestream is broadcasting the next supernatural disaster in real time.

The perspective cuts out of the livestream into a wide shot of the sky, where a red aura twists into a violent wind. Bone fragments, shadows, and the grief of the missing dead pull together to form the Gashadokuro, a massive kaiju-sized skeleton yokai that screams over the town and begins rampaging through the streets. Hana is suddenly overtaken by Aoi’s spirit, not fully possessed in a villainous way but forced into a prophetic state. Through Hana, Aoi warns that this is the point of no return: the scattered spirits, the bells, the collar, and the town’s false legend are all being pulled toward the shrine. Aoi tells Hana that the true evil has been controlling events from beneath the shrine and begs her not to repeat the choice Aoi made in the past. When Hana regains enough control, she summons Kuro through the spiritual leash and commands him to take her to the shrine.

Kuro obeys immediately, pulling Hana onto his bosozoku-style motorcycle and racing toward the shrine as the Gashadokuro notices her movement and begins following. Hana sees Ren nearby and shouts for him to call on the full power of the Spirit Wardens. Ren steps into the skeleton’s path and finally uses a higher-order Warden technique tied to the Silver Requiem Violin. His music opens a silver ritual channel, and he undergoes a magical transformation into a mecha-pilot-style Spirit Warden battle outfit. The sequence cuts to the Spirit Warden orbital headquarters, where a massive silver musical mecha is loaded like a sacred weapon and launched toward Earth. Ghostly white lightning strikes the town The zord lands in front of the Gashadokuro, and Ren jumps into the cockpit with the Silver Saber expanding into the mecha’s primary blade.

The battle between Ren’s silver mecha and the Gashadokuro becomes the arc’s huge spectacle set piece. The mecha fights with elegant music-based attacks, silver blade strikes, and violin-note energy blasts, while the skeleton smashes through buildings, screams and tries to claw its way toward Hana. For a moment, Ren appears to gain the upper hand, using the giant Silver Saber to cut through the skeleton’s ribs and expose the blue glow of the Bone Bell inside its chest. But just when it seems like Ren might win, the Gashadokuro counters with a brutal strike, driving a massive bone spike through the silver mecha. The mecha staggers, sparks and silver light pouring from the wound, and Ren is left trapped inside as the skeleton turns away from him. The arc ends with Kuro and Hana arriving at the shrine, looking back as the Gashadokuro begins moving toward them — the Bone Bell still unrecovered, Ren seemingly defeated, and the shrine now directly in the monster’s path.

ARC 9 — THE STOLEN BONE BELL
Bell: Bell 8 — The Bone Bell, revealed from the destroyed Gashadokuro but intercepted before reaching Kuro’s collar
Minor Villain: Possessed Principal Hoshino, acting as Mawari’s human vessel
Major Threat: Mawari’s influence begins openly possessing humans in positions of power
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Saki Hoshino, Principal Hoshino, Aoi
New Character / Presence Introduced: Principal Hoshino’s possessed form as Mawari’s active agent
Arc Motivation: Hana and Kuro must survive the Gashadokuro’s arrival at the shrine, but the greater danger becomes the possessed principal stealing the Bone Bell and manipulating Ren into turning against Kuro.
Arc-End Twist: Ren is tricked into believing Kuro has lost control and raises the Silver Saber to kill him, while the possessed principal remains free with the stolen bell.

Act 9 begins with the Gashadokuro reaching the shrine, towering over Hana and Kuro as if there is no hope left. Ren’s silver mecha has been badly damaged, the town is in chaos, and the skeleton is now close enough to crush the shrine entrance. Aoi communicates through Hana and tells her that to save herself is within her power; she must channel the power hidden in her own spiritual line. Hana draws on the ancient power of Aoi, the Mori bloodline, unleashing a massive blast of blue-white spiritual energy that tears through the Gashadokuro and destroys it. The skeleton collapses into bone dust and red shadow, revealing the Bone Bell inside its chest. As the bell begins flying toward Kuro’s collar, a hand snatches it out of the air.

The hand belongs to Principal Hoshino, but he is no longer hiding behind his polished school-authority mask. A black-red aura rolls off him, showing clear signs of demonic possession and Mawari’s influence. Saki arrives still livestreaming, shocked to see her father standing in the middle of the shrine disaster with the glowing bell in his hand. She turns the camera on him and demands to know what is happening, then immediately blames Hana for bringing this nightmare into their lives. The principal’s aura grows darker, and without hesitation he violently swipes Saki aside, knocking her unconscious. Her phone drops to the ground and keeps streaming a broken view of the scene, catching fragments of her father’s demonic aura, Hana’s mark, and Kuro’s collar.

Powered by Mawari’s influence and the stolen Bone Bell, Principal Hoshino launches himself at Hana. Kuro intercepts him, forcing a brutal fight at the shrine entrance while Hana tries to support Kuro through their bond. Hana and Kuro briefly fight in sync: Hana channels warding energy through her mark while Kuro uses blue flame and claws to drive the possessed principal back. For a moment, they overpower him together, knocking him to the ground. But the principal strikes back at Hana with a burst of black-red energy, knocking her unconscious and leaving Kuro momentarily unbalanced by rage. This is exactly the opening Mawari needs.

Ren arrives battered but alive, no longer in the mecha system and back in his normal clothes, carrying the Silver Requiem Violin and Silver Saber. The possessed principal instantly shifts tactics, acting wounded and human again while spinning the story that Kuro has turned on everyone. He tells Ren that Hana is unconscious because Kuro lost control, and that if Ren truly wants to protect her, he must do what the Wardens trained him to do. Ren sees Kuro’s unstable demon state, Hana on the ground, Saki unconscious nearby, and the shrine in ruins. His worst fear clicks into place: Kuro is too dangerous to remain alive. Even though the audience knows the principal is manipulating him, Ren believes he is making the only choice left.

Ren attacks Kuro, accusing him of losing control and saying that if Kuro really wants to save Hana, he has to die by Ren’s hand. Kuro tries to warn him that the principal has the bell, but Ren is too emotionally triggered to listen. Their fight becomes the emotional rupture the story has been building toward: silver music and the Silver Saber against blue flame, claws, and collar chains. While they battle, both boys lose focus on Principal Hoshino, who quietly regains control of the stolen Bone Bell. Ren finally knocks Kuro to the ground and raises the Silver Saber for a killing blow. The arc ends with Hana unconscious, Saki’s livestream still running, the possessed principal holding the Bone Bell, and Ren poised to kill Kuro in front of the shrine.


ARC 10 — THE BROKEN BELLS
Bell: Bell 8 — The Bone Bell restored; Bell 9 — The Heart Bell summoned from Hana
Major Villain: Mawari, the corruption spirit behind the shrine legend
Human Vessel: Possessed Principal Hoshino
Main Characters: Hana Mori, Kuro Aonuma, Ren Shirogane, Aoi, Principal Hoshino, Mawari, Mina Park, Daigo Shirogane
New Character / Presence Introduced: Mawari’s true spiritual form and the full truth of Aoi’s past
Arc Motivation: Hana must break the repeating cycle between herself, Aoi, Kuro, and Mawari before the completed bells turn Kuro into a leashed demon weapon again.
Arc-End Resolution: Hana destroys the bells instead of completing the collar, freeing Kuro and Aoi but severing the spiritual bond that made Kuro remember her.

Arc 10 begins with Kuro and Ren locked in a brutal, emotional fight at the shrine, picking up from Ren’s attempted killing blow. Ren believes Kuro has lost control and that killing him is the only way to save Hana, while Kuro is furious that Ren keeps using protection as an excuse to control her. Their battle is personal and desperate: Silver Saber against claws, violin magic against blue flame, two boys who both love Hana trying to prove the other is the real danger. Kuro eventually overpowers and incapacitates Ren, but he is exhausted, unstable, and still not strong enough to face the possessed Principal Hoshino. The principal rises again, now glowing with Mawari’s black-red aura, and makes it clear that Kuro was never the final monster — he was only the vessel Mawari wanted.

Aoi urges Hana to use the power awakening inside her to destroy Mawari. Hana channels Aoi’s spiritual force and blasts the possessed principal, separating his human body from the evil spirit controlling him. At first, it looks like Hana has won: the principal collapses, the black-red corruption tears away from him, and Mawari’s spirit form is exposed. But then Hana begins absorbing the released power through Aoi, and something inside her changes. Her memories unlock, not just as visions but as a full spiritual inheritance from Aoi’s lifetime.

The arc then flashes back to the past, where Aoi and Kuro once fought an avatar of Mawari in a feudal-era battlefield of fire, broken shrines, and war. Aoi believed she had defeated Mawari and absorbed the mysterious power left behind, thinking it would help her protect Kuro and the town. The truth is that the power was Mawari itself: a corruption that enters those who willingly take it in. Once Aoi absorbed it, Mawari began twisting her love, fear, and desire for Kuro into possession. The bells and collar were created to control Kuro’s demon form, but under Mawari’s influence, Aoi used them to turn Kuro into a leashed beast.

The real tragedy is revealed: Aoi, corrupted by Mawari, used Kuro to commit atrocities in the past. Kuro eventually broke through the control of the bells and saw what Mawari had done through Aoi. To free her from Mawari’s influence, Kuro had to kill the woman he loved. That act severed his spiritual tether to the world, erased his memory, and left him wandering across time as an almost-immortal guardian with only fragments of memory that recall pain and longing. The ninth bell was not lost; it passed from Aoi into her reincarnated soul, meaning it has always been inside Hana.

Hana realizes this cycle has repeated across lifetimes because Aoi’s mistake was not loving Kuro — it was trying to keep him through power. Hana knows she loves Kuro and always will, but she also understands that controlling him, even to save him, would repeat Mawari’s corruption. Marwari is the incarnation of despair, fear, and shame. feeding on people’s negative emotions to grow his power and influence. If Hana absorbs Mawari’s power and completes the collar, she and Kuro will become what Aoi and Kuro once became: a corrupted priestess and her leashed demon weapon. The choice Aoi kept warning about is finally clear: Hana can have Kuro forever by controlling him, or she can free him and risk losing him.

Hana takes the stolen eighth bell and lets it enter Kuro’s collar. The transformation is overwhelming and intimate: the Bone Bell locks into place, Kuro’s body surges with blue light, and the spiritual chain between him and Hana pulls them closer. Mawari circles them in spirit form, pressing in like a shadow, urging Hana to summon the ninth bell and finish the cycle. Hana nearly gives in because the bond is intoxicating, painful, and full of everything she wants: memory, desire, protection, and Kuro beside her forever. The ninth bell begins to emerge from her chest, and Mawari believes it has won.

At the last moment, Hana wakes from the trance and refuses the easy path. Instead of completing the collar, she uses Aoi’s spiritual power to destroy the bells one by one. Each bell shatters with a flash of memory: the shrine, the battlefield, Aoi’s kiss, Kuro’s chains, the dead, the lies, the centuries of control. Mawari screams in disbelief as Hana breaks the system it has used to trap them across time. With only two bells left, Mawari tries to bargain, telling Hana that if she stops, she can have Kuro by her side forever. It warns her that if she breaks the collar completely, Kuro will forget her and it will be as if they never met.

Hana chooses freedom anyway. As she destroys the second-to-last bell, Aoi appears beside her, finally understanding what she could not do in life. Aoi admits she acted out of love, but her love was blinded by fear and possession. She tells Hana that letting Kuro go is the only way to truly save him and the world. This realization frees Aoi’s soul at last. Hana destroys the final bell, and Mawari’s form tears apart in a scream of black-red light. For one suspended moment, Kuro and Hana embrace as the collar and chains all shatter around them.

The story cuts to a new morning. Hana walks to school happy and light, as if a weight has been lifted from the town and from her soul. Ren and Daigo drive by, and Ren shares a quiet passing glance with Hana, suggesting some emotional residue remains even if the supernatural cycle is broken. Mina joins Hana and jokes that she was staring too long at that boy, pulling the story back into playful school life. When they arrive at school, Mina says everyone is talking about a new boy who just transferred in. A motorcycle roars up, and Kuro pulls in like the first day all over again. Mina jokes that he is her type, Hana teases her back, and then Hana and Kuro look at each other. They do not fully remember, but something in them recognizes the other, and Hana smiles.
