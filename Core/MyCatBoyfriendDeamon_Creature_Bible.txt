Creature Bible
My Cat Boyfriend Is the Town Demon
________________


1. Mawari, the Shadow Under the Shrine
Creature Type: Ancient shadow demon / corrupted nekomata-like shrine entity
Appears In: Hinted throughout all arcs; fully revealed in Arc 10
Number: One true entity, but it can speak through shadows, mirrors, possessed humans, spirits, and corrupted objects.
Mawari is the true town demon sealed beneath the old shrine. For generations, the town has blamed Kuro as the monster, but Kuro is actually the living lock holding Mawari back. Mawari feeds on secrets, shame, jealousy, forbidden love, gossip, betrayal, and emotional repression. It does not usually attack directly at first; it manipulates people into lying, hiding, desiring, betraying, and humiliating one another until smaller spirits are born from that emotional corruption.
Visually, Mawari appears as black-red shadow with catlike movement, red eyes in reflective surfaces, and eventually a massive nekomata-like silhouette with too many tails. Its voice should feel intimate and poisonous, often using private truths to make characters believe false conclusions. Mawari’s greatest goal is to make Hana’s love for Kuro ring the ninth bell willingly, because that emotional bond can open the shrine and allow Mawari to possess Kuro’s body. Its weakness is honest, freely chosen love that does not control, possess, or sacrifice another person.

2. Jikininki Revenants
Creature Type: Corpse-eating revenant spirits / Japanese zombie-like shrine dead
Appears In: Arc 1 — The Shrine Mark
Number: A group of 5–8 revenants in the shrine attack sequence, led by one stronger bell-bearing revenant.
The Jikininki Revenants are corpse-like spirits that attack Hana inside the abandoned shrine at the start of the series. They look like dead students or townspeople with gray skin, cracked jaws, sunken eyes, and broken movements, as if their bodies are being dragged by hunger instead of life. They are not intelligent in a normal human way; they are hunger spirits animated by Mawari’s influence and drawn to anyone connected to the seal. Their main function is to create the immediate action opening and force Kuro to reveal his true blue cat-demon form.
The lead revenant carries the first Moonbell inside its mouth, suggesting Mawari has been hiding pieces of Kuro’s collar inside corrupted spirits. When destroyed, the revenants can briefly reassemble unless the bell is removed or their hunger-core is burned by Kuro’s blue flame. Their weakness is direct purification through blue flame or shrine bells. Their presence establishes that the shrine is not abandoned — it is infected.

3. Tama
Creature Type: Shrine cat familiar
Appears In: Arc 1 onward
Number: One
Tama is not a villain, but he is a supernatural creature and recurring guide. He appears as an old black cat with a torn ear and a small blue bell collar. He can speak to Hana after she receives the curse mark, though most ordinary humans only hear meows. Tama is ancient, sarcastic, suspicious of humans, and deeply protective of Kuro even when he pretends not to care.
Tama once belonged to Aoi, the first shrine maiden, which makes him a living bridge between the original sealing and Hana’s present story. He knows more than he reveals and often gives warnings in the most irritating way possible. He is useful for short-form storytelling because he can appear suddenly before danger, hiss at invisible spirits, or physically react to lies. His weakness is emotional guilt: he knows the truth about Kuro’s suffering but has spent years helping preserve the seal.

4. Noppera-bō, the Faceless Student
Creature Type: Faceless ghost / identity-stealing yokai
Appears In: Arc 2 — The Faceless Student
Number: One primary Noppera-bō, but it can create 2–3 false face copies during the battle.
The Noppera-bō appears at school by stealing Riku Tanaka’s face and walking through the hallway wearing blank skin. It is born from social fear: the terror of being watched, judged, exposed, and stripped of identity. It can imitate voices and repeat secrets that its victims never said aloud. This makes it perfect for a school setting where reputation is everything.
The Noppera-bō attacks by removing faces, confusing witnesses, and creating copies that wear familiar identities. It can appear as Hana, Kuro, Mina, or Riku to destabilize the group. Its weakness is recognition: if Hana can identify the original lie or hidden fear behind its borrowed face, the copy starts to crack. Ren’s Silver Requiem Violin can bind it, while Kuro’s blue flame can destroy its false bodies.

5. Teke Teke / The Half-Girl in the Hall
Creature Type: Crawling school spirit / vengeful urban legend yokai
Appears In: Arc 3 — The Half-Girl in the Hall
Number: One main spirit
The Teke Teke is a half-bodied school spirit that drags herself through the third-floor hallway using her hands. She moves fast, scraping across floors, walls, lockers, and even ceilings. Her sound signature is the sharp “teke teke” rhythm of nails and elbows hitting tile. She represents severance: cut friendships, broken reputations, split identities, and the fear of being publicly destroyed.
This spirit attacks physically, making her one of the more action-forward minor villains. She slices lockers, corners students, and forces Kuro and Ren into their first real combat collaboration. The third Moonbell is hidden in her severed school badge, tying her to student identity and school trauma. Her weakness is coordinated restraint: Ren must slow her with silver music while Kuro pins her with blue flame long enough for Hana to retrieve the bell.

6. Ubume / The Rain Mother
Creature Type: Grieving mother spirit / rain yokai
Status: Cut from current Arc Outline / reserved for possible future use.
Note: The original Rain Mother Arc 4 was replaced by Arc 4 — Emi’s House / The Mirror Cat Breach. The current season arc does not use Ubume or the Rain Mother Bell.
Appears In: Not used in the current season arc.
Number: One main spirit, with rainwater extensions that can appear in mirrors, windows, puddles, and indoor water.
The Ubume is a sorrowful rain spirit who appears holding an empty baby blanket. She is drawn to Keiko Mori’s sealed memory and to Hana’s childhood connection to the shrine. She does not begin as purely evil; she is a grief-spirit twisted by Mawari’s influence and by memories traded away for silence. Her horror comes from maternal loss, forgotten children, and the idea that a parent can choose to forget something unbearable.
The Ubume attacks internally and spiritually rather than only physically. She causes Keiko to sleepwalk toward the shrine, floods rooms from the inside, and forces buried memories to leak through mirrors. Her fourth Moonbell is hidden inside the empty baby blanket, making the bell feel like a missing child or lost memory. Her weakness is compassionate recognition: Hana must recover the memory without destroying Keiko’s mind, while Ren’s Lullaby Seal and Kuro’s protection hold the rain spirit back.

7. Ungaikyō / Mirror Cat
Creature Type: Mirror yokai merged with bakeneko-style copycat spirit
Appears In: Arcs 4–5 — The Mirror Cat Breach / The Mirror School
Number: One central Mirror Cat with two mirror-cores, creating multiple reflected copies of Kuro, Hana, Ren, and Aoi.
The Mirror Cat is a seductive and psychologically dangerous spirit that lives in reflective surfaces. It can copy the face and voice of the person someone desires most, then tell them exactly what they want to hear. For Hana, it appears as a gentler Kuro who finally admits his feelings, nearly tricking her into a kiss. Its function is to weaponize longing, insecurity, and romantic confusion.
The Mirror Cat is not physically powerful at first, but it is emotionally surgical. It uses reflections to show Hana possible futures: Ren as safety, Kuro as danger, Aoi as destiny, and Hana as someone losing herself. It carries two mirrored Moonbells because the original Reflection Bell split into two spiritual halves: Bell 4, the False Reflection Bell, and Bell 5, the True Reflection Bell. The False Reflection Bell is released when Emi and Ren crack the Mirror Cat’s first mirror-core at Emi’s house by rejecting fear-based illusions. The True Reflection Bell is hidden deeper inside the Mirror Cat’s second core and is released at school when Hana refuses the emotionally comforting lie. Its weakness is refusal of illusion: first fear, then comfort.

8. Aoi’s Reflection
Creature Type: Ghost fragment / shrine maiden soul echo
Appears In: Arc 5 onward
Number: One soul fragment, but she appears through mirrors, water, dreams, and Hana’s mark.
Aoi is not a simple villain, but she functions as a supernatural obstacle and emotional antagonist for Hana. She was the first shrine maiden who loved Kuro and sealed him. Her reflection appears wearing Hana’s face, making Hana question whether her love for Kuro is real or inherited. Aoi’s presence complicates the romance because she represents the previous tragic version of Hana and Kuro’s bond.
Aoi is a ghost fragment tied to Hana’s curse mark and the Moonbell Collar. She can show memories, warn Hana, or manipulate her through guilt. At first, she seems like a romantic rival from the past, but later she becomes a tragic guide. Her weakness is confession: she must admit that she sealed Kuro partly out of fear, not only sacrifice.

9. Yoru
Creature Type: Rogue cat-demon / soul-fragment entity
Appears In: Arc 5 onward
Number: One
Yoru is a dangerous rogue cat-demon with one yellow eye and one blue eye. He claims to be Kuro’s brother, but the truth is stranger: he may have been created from the first bell Kuro lost, making him part of Kuro’s original soul. He moves like a stray cat, appears in windows, rooftops, and alleys, and speaks with playful cruelty. Yoru is not the main villain, but he acts as a chaos agent and recurring supernatural sub-villain.
Yoru’s motivation is to separate Kuro from Hana and push Kuro back toward his demon nature. He believes humans only use monsters and that Kuro has become weak by protecting them. He often tells the truth in the most damaging way possible, making him dangerous even when he is technically helping. His weakness is abandonment: beneath the cruelty, he wants Kuro to acknowledge him as part of himself.

10. Biwa-bokuboku, the Instrument That Hates Music
Creature Type: Tsukumogami / corrupted living instrument spirit
Appears In: Arc 6 — The Warden’s Song
Number: One main corrupted instrument spirit
The Biwa-bokuboku is a possessed old instrument from the abandoned school music room. It has a warped wooden body, strings like human hair, and a mouth-like crack that sings Spirit Warden melodies in distorted form. It is a tsukumogami — an object spirit — corrupted by Mawari’s access to stolen Warden secrets. Its presence reveals that Mawari knows techniques the Wardens believed were hidden.
This creature attacks through sound, making it a perfect enemy for Ren. It counters his violin techniques, echoes his childhood training songs, and turns music into pain. It forces Ren to question whether his family’s sacred exorcist music has always been pure or whether it was built on stolen shrine knowledge. Its weakness is dissonance recognition: Hana hears the true bell tone hidden underneath its corrupted melody, allowing Kuro and Ren to expose the sixth Moonbell.

11. Jorōgumo, the Spider Woman of the Bell Festival
Creature Type: Spider woman yokai / desire-feeding predator
Appears In: Arc 7 — The Spider Festival
Number: One main Jorōgumo, plus multiple silk-controlled student thralls.
The Jorōgumo appears at the town Bell Festival disguised as a beautiful woman selling red thread bracelets. She feeds on jealousy, romantic insecurity, public embarrassment, and possessive desire. She targets Saki by offering her the power to make someone choose her, then uses black spider silk to control her body and emotions. This makes the Jorōgumo a perfect villain for the love-triangle and school-status portion of the story.
Her attacks are both physical and emotional. She binds students with invisible red and black threads, forces public romantic humiliation, and manipulates Kuro into appearing to betray Hana. The seventh Moonbell is woven into her web above the festival bell tower. Her weakness is severance of false desire: Ren’s Silver Saber cuts the threads, Kuro burns the web, and Hana must resist jealousy long enough to retrieve the bell.

12. Spider-Thralls
Creature Type: Human victims controlled by Jorōgumo silk
Appears In: Arc 7
Number: 8–20 festivalgoers and students can be briefly controlled, depending on production scale.
Spider-thralls are ordinary students or townspeople bound by the Jorōgumo’s invisible silk. They do not look fully possessed at first; their smiles become too fixed, their movements too synchronized, and their wrists show faint red thread marks. They act out hidden desires, jealousy, and resentment. This allows the festival to become dangerous without requiring every threat to be a full creature.
The thralls are useful for crowd pressure and quick vertical-drama danger. They can surround Hana, block Kuro’s path, or turn on Mina while still looking like recognizable classmates. They are not evil and should not be killed. Their weakness is thread-cutting, either through the Silver Saber or Kuro’s controlled blue flame.

13. Gashadokuro, the Giant Bone Shadow
Creature Type: Giant skeleton yokai formed from the dead and missing
Appears In: Arc 8 — The Giant Bone Shadow
Number: One massive entity, made from dozens of trapped souls and bones.
The Gashadokuro is a towering skeleton made from the grief, hunger, and remains of missing townspeople. It appears beneath Shrine Hill in the old tunnel system, where Mawari has hidden those swallowed by the town’s silence. Its skull whispers the names of the missing, including people connected to Mina, Hana, and Haru. This creature turns the mystery of disappearances into a physical monster.
Unlike earlier spirits, the Gashadokuro is a large-scale physical threat. It can crush tunnels, form skeletal hands from walls, and reform when broken because it is held together by unresolved grief. Kuro’s blue flame can damage it, but burning it carelessly risks harming the trapped souls. Its weakness is release: Hana must hear the voices inside it, Ren must sever the grief chains, and Kuro must burn only the hunger core.

14. Jun Park, Half-Shadow Messenger
Creature Type: Human survivor partially altered by shadow possession
Status: Cut from current Arc Outline / reserved for possible future use.
Note: Jun’s missing-brother plot is not being used in the current season arc. Mina remains important as Hana’s best friend, school-world anchor, and emotional witness, but Jun’s shadow-messenger thread is deferred.
Appears In: Not used in the current season arc.
Number: One
Jun Park is Mina’s missing older brother, returned as a half-shadow messenger. He is not a villain, but his condition makes him a frightening supernatural figure when he first appears. One of his eyes is filled with black shadow, and his body sometimes flickers like a bad video signal. He has survived inside Mawari’s network by carrying messages from the trapped dead, including Haru.
Jun’s presence personalizes the missing-people plot. He proves that Mawari does not simply kill victims; it uses them as messengers, bait, memory containers, or living evidence. He may lose control if Mawari pulls on the shadow inside him. His weakness is emotional anchoring, especially Mina’s voice and memories from before he disappeared.

15. Haru Mori, Blue-Flame Prisoner
Creature Type: Human spirit-marked survivor / partial shrine prisoner
Status: Cut from current Arc Outline / reserved for possible future use.
Note: Haru’s missing-father mystery is not being paid off in the current season arc. For now, the arc focuses on Hana, Kuro, Aoi, Ren, the bells, and Mawari.
Appears In: Not used in the current season arc.
Number: One
Haru Mori is Hana’s missing father, but when he returns, he carries supernatural contamination from years inside Mawari’s shadow network. He has blue flame burning across his fingers, and he appears unchanged from the night he vanished. At first, Kuro suspects he may be a shadow wearing Haru’s face. This uncertainty makes Haru function as both a revelation and a possible threat when he first appears.
Haru is not a villain, but Mawari has used his voice, reflection, and memory to manipulate Hana. His body carries knowledge of the old seal, the Moonbell Collar, and the truth that Kuro is the lock. He is dangerous because his love for Hana once led him to bargain with Mawari. His weakness is guilt: he fears Hana will hate him for the choices he made to save her.

16. Hashihime, the Jealous Bridge Spirit
Creature Type: Jealous bridge yokai / vengeance bride spirit
Appears In: Arc 9 — The Jealous Bridge
Number: One
Hashihime is a bride-like spirit who haunts the old bridge below Shrine Hill. She feeds on jealousy, romantic fear, possessiveness, and the pain of not being chosen. Her power is strongest at thresholds, especially bridges, because bridges symbolize crossing from one emotional state to another. She is the perfect minor villain for the arc where Hana, Kuro, and Ren must finally confront the truth of the love triangle.
Hashihime does not invent emotions; she amplifies what is already hidden. She makes Kuro see Hana choosing Ren, Ren see Hana kissing Kuro, and Hana see Kuro returning to Aoi. Her attacks turn love into violence by making characters act on fear rather than truth. Her weakness is honest confession: when Hana admits she cares for Ren but is falling in love with Kuro, the spirit’s power cracks because the jealousy is no longer hidden.

17. Kasha, the Fire-Cart Gatekeeper
Creature Type: Fire-cart corpse spirit / underworld yokai
Appears In: Arc 10 — The Final Bell
Number: One main Kasha, possibly accompanied by 2–3 smaller corpse-flame wheels if more action is needed.
The Kasha is Mawari’s gatekeeper beneath the shrine. It appears as a flaming corpse-cart or monstrous fire spirit dragging burning wheels through the underground shrine tunnel. In folklore-inspired terms, it is associated with stealing bodies and carrying souls away, making it ideal as the final threshold monster before Hana reaches Kuro. It is not subtle; it is a loud, physical, terrifying guardian designed to stop rescue.
The Kasha attacks with burning wheels, corpse-flame, and sudden charges through narrow tunnels. Ren’s silver music struggles against it because its flames consume sound, forcing the team to improvise. Yoru can distract it because he is partly made from Kuro’s lost bell-energy, giving him a sacrificial or redemptive moment. Its weakness is misdirection and spiritual overload: it can be lured away from the main path and burned out by feeding on a false bell signal.

18. Souta, the First Bellmaker’s Ghost
Creature Type: Ancestral ghost / shrine artisan spirit
Appears In: Arc 10
Number: One
Souta is the ghost of the first bellmaker who helped create the original Moonbell Collar. He appears beneath the shrine near the broken altar, surrounded by faint blue bell-light. He is not a villain, but he is a supernatural entity who reveals the true purpose of the bells. His role is to clarify that the bells were meant to one day free Kuro, not imprison him forever.
Souta’s presence gives the finale spiritual authority without overexplaining the mythology. He explains that the final bell cannot be purified through sacrifice, ownership, or control. It must ring through freely chosen love. His weakness is limitation: he can reveal truth, but he cannot fight Mawari or make Hana’s choice for her.

19. Rumor Moths
Creature Type: Lesser shadow spirits born from gossip
Appears In: Can appear across school arcs, especially Arcs 2, 3, and 7
Number: Swarms of 10–50 tiny spirits, depending on the intensity of the rumor.
Rumor Moths are small black-blue moth-like spirits that hatch from gossip, screenshots, anonymous posts, and whispered secrets. They crawl out of phone speakers, locker vents, classroom windows, and torn paper charms. Individually, they are weak, but in a swarm they can overwhelm a victim with whispers until the person believes the worst possible interpretation of a situation. They are useful recurring signs that Mawari’s influence is spreading through the school.
Rumor Moths do not need a full episode as main villains, but they can escalate scenes quickly. Mina’s phone charm may react when they appear, making her a warning system. Their weakness is exposure: when a truth is spoken clearly or a lie is publicly disproven, the moths burn away like paper ash. They visually connect gossip culture to supernatural infestation.

20. Shadow Thralls
Creature Type: Humans temporarily possessed or influenced by Mawari’s shadow
Appears In: Throughout the series, especially Arcs 7–10
Number: Varies from one person to small groups of 3–12.
Shadow Thralls are humans whose emotions have been amplified by Mawari until they become temporary vessels. They can be students, teachers, festivalgoers, Wardens, or townspeople. They do not always look monstrous; often their eyes briefly flash red in reflections while their real faces remain normal. This makes them useful for paranoia because Hana cannot immediately know who is being influenced.
Shadow Thralls act on their own worst impulses: jealousy, fear, ambition, guilt, or loyalty to a lie. They are not fully innocent, because Mawari uses what is already inside them, but they are not pure monsters either. Their weakness is emotional interruption: calling out the truth, breaking the secret, or severing the curse object controlling them. Ren can suppress them with Lullaby Seal, while Hana can sometimes hear the original person trapped beneath the shadow.

21. Mirror Echoes
Creature Type: Mawari-linked reflection spirits
Appears In: Arcs 4, 5, 8, 9, and 10
Number: Many small manifestations; usually one active reflection per scene.
Mirror Echoes are not full yokai but fragments of Mawari’s sight inside reflective surfaces. They appear in mirrors, phone screens, windows, puddles, clinic glass, and polished floors. They can show real memories, false futures, or people who are not physically present. This makes them one of Mawari’s most useful tools for manipulating Hana.
Mirror Echoes often mix truth and lie. For example, Haru may appear in a phone reflection with a real warning, but Mawari may distort the timing or emotional context. They should feel intimate and invasive, as if the world is watching Hana from every reflective surface. Their weakness is direct light, broken glass, or rejecting the emotional bait they offer.

22. Bell-Wraiths
Creature Type: Residual spirits attached to recovered Moonbells
Appears In: Can appear briefly whenever a bell is recovered
Number: One echo per bell, not always visible.
Bell-Wraiths are emotional imprints left inside each recovered Moonbell. They are not independent villains, but they can attack, tempt, or reveal memory when the bell is touched. Each Bell-Wraith carries the dominant emotion of its bell: hunger, identity, severance, grief, memory, control, desire, buried bones, or heart-love. They allow the story to turn each bell recovery into a mini-revelation instead of a simple object pickup.
Bell-Wraiths appear as flashes: Aoi’s hand sealing Kuro, Haru’s voice in static, a child crying in rain, or Kuro’s human hand reaching through chains. They can overwhelm Kuro or Hana if too many memories return too quickly. Their weakness is integration: once the truth of the bell is accepted, the wraith quiets and the bell locks into place. They are essential to making the Moonbell quest emotionally serialized.
