# My Cat Boyfriend Deamon

Project bible and script workspace for **My Cat Boyfriend Is the Town Demon**, a vertical microdrama fantasy-romance series designed for short mobile episodes.

## Project Overview

This project is for developing a supernatural vertical show episode by episode. The story follows Hana Mori, a shrine-marked high school girl, and Kuro Aonuma, a cursed blue cat-demon guardian blamed for the town's disasters. As Hana uncovers the truth behind Kuro's Moonbell Collar, the Spirit Wardens, and the demon Mawari beneath the shrine, the romance becomes dangerous because love, control, memory, and possession are all tied to the curse.

The intended format is a fast, cliffhanger-driven microdrama:

- Vertical mobile-first episodes
- Roughly 1-2 minutes per episode
- Estimated season length: about 52 episodes
- Strong hooks, quick turns, visual conflict, and frequent cliffhangers
- Scripts should be detailed enough to support later AI video generation

## Episode Writing Rules

Do not start writing episodes until explicitly instructed to write an episode.

When episode writing begins:

- Write all episodes into `Core/MyCatBoyfriendDeamon_Script.txt`.
- Preserve continuity with the character bible, creature bible, worldbuilding file, and arc outline.
- Treat the arc outline as the active story source of truth.
- Keep each episode focused on one major turn: plot advance, stakes rise, or relationship shift.
- End every episode with a hook, reveal, threat, reversal, or unresolved emotional beat.
- Keep dialogue short and pressure-driven.
- Use detailed visual descriptions for characters, locations, actions, props, creature behavior, and atmosphere.

## AI Video Generation Notes

The scripts are intended to be converted later into generative AI video using **Seedance 2.0**.

Because of that, episode scripts should include production-useful detail:

- Clear location descriptions
- Character appearance and body language
- Creature design and movement
- Camera-relevant action and staging
- Important props and magical objects
- Lighting, weather, reflections, shadows, blue flame, and black-red Mawari energy
- Vertical-friendly visual beats that can be understood quickly on mobile

The writing should still read like a fast microdrama script, not a prose chapter.

## Active Continuity Notes

- Kuro does not speak normally. He communicates through meows, growls, roars, posture, claws, eye contact, and catlike body language.
- Hana usually interprets Kuro's communication for the audience.
- Ren cannot understand Kuro directly until their cooperation develops later.
- The Rain Mother, Haru Mori, and Jun Park threads are currently cut from the active season arc and reserved for possible future use.
- The Mirror Cat has two mirror-cores, releasing Bell 4, the False Reflection Bell, and Bell 5, the True Reflection Bell.
- The Orbital Requiem / Final Solution is seeded in Arc 6 and pays off in Arc 8.

## Working Process

Use the core files for planning, continuity checks, arc refinement, and episode scripting. Make continuity changes only when requested or approved.

When reviewing or revising the project:

- Check the arc against the character, creature, and worldbuilding files.
- Flag contradictions before changing files.
- Keep cut material marked as cut/reserved rather than deleting it unless explicitly asked.
- Prioritize microdrama momentum: immediate hooks, emotional escalation, visual clarity, and cliffhangers.
